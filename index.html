Rupali Bank Ltd., a financial institution with a rich legacy, was established on March 26, 1972, through the merger of three erstwhile commercial banks: Muslim Commercial Bank Ltd., Australasia Bank Ltd., and Standard Bank Ltd. The formation was executed under the Bangladesh Banks (Nationalization) Order 1972 (P.O. No. 26 of 1972), consolidating assets, benefits, rights, and liabilities of the predecessor banks. Operating as a nationalized commercial bank until December 13, 1986, Rupali Bank emerged as the largest Public Limited Banking Company in Bangladesh on December 14, 1986. Presently, Rupali Bank boasts an authorized capital of Tk. 7000 million (US$ 88.66 million) and a paid-up capital of Tk. 2760.39 million (US$ 35.39 million), with a capital structure comprising 90.19% government shareholding and 09.81% private shareholding. The bank's total number of shares, each valued at Tk. 10, amounts to 276,038,812, and as of July 25, 2016, 275,452,502 shares have been dematerialized by shareholders. Rupali Bank operates a vast network of 586 branches, maintaining global connectivity through its foreign correspondents. With a dedicated team of 7,164 employees, the bank is committed to providing a comprehensive range of financial services. The helm of Rupali Bank is guided by a seasoned professional banker serving as the Managing Director & CEO, ensuring strategic leadership and continued growth in the ever-evolving financial landscape. The bank's financial statements reflect its commitment to transparency and excellence, underlining its pivotal role in Bangladesh's banking sector.

Mohammad Jahangir

Managing Director & CEO
